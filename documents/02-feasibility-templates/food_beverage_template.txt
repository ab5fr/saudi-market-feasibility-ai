## Food & Beverage Business Specific Considerations

This template provides guidance for conducting feasibility studies for restaurants, cafes, food trucks, and catering businesses in Saudi Arabia.

## Regulatory Requirements

### Licenses Required
1. **Commercial Registration (CR)** - Ministry of Commerce
2. **Municipal License (Balady)** - Local municipality
3. **Food Safety Certificate** - Saudi Food & Drug Authority (SFDA)
4. **Health Cards** - All food handlers
5. **Signage Permit** - Municipality
6. **Music License** (if applicable) - General Entertainment Authority

### Health & Safety Requirements
- HACCP certification recommended
- Regular health inspections
- Proper waste management system
- Food storage compliance
- Kitchen ventilation standards
- Fire safety compliance

### Revenue Projections

**Conservative Estimate (First Year):**
- Average transaction: SAR 45-80
- Daily customers: 80-150
- Monthly revenue: SAR 108,000-360,000
- Annual revenue: SAR 1.3M-4.3M

**Growth Projections:**
- Year 2: +25-40% revenue growth
- Year 3: +15-25% revenue growth (stabilizes)

### Break-Even Analysis

Typical food & beverage businesses in Saudi Arabia:
- **Break-even period:** 12-24 months
- **Average ROI:** 20-35% by Year 3
- **Profit margin:** 10-20% net (after Year 1)

## Market Analysis

### Target Customer Segments

1. **Young Professionals (25-35)**
   - Frequency: 2-3 times per week
   - Average spend: SAR 60-100
   - Preferences: Quick service, WiFi, Instagram-worthy

2. **Families**
   - Frequency: 1-2 times per week
   - Average spend: SAR 200-400 per visit
   - Preferences: Kid-friendly, comfortable seating, value meals

3. **Students**
   - Frequency: 3-5 times per week
   - Average spend: SAR 30-60
   - Preferences: Affordable, study-friendly, WiFi

4. **Business Meetings**
   - Frequency: 2-3 times per week
   - Average spend: SAR 80-150
   - Preferences: Quiet environment, premium service

### Market Trends (2024)

- **Specialty Coffee:** Growing 15% annually
- **Healthy Food:** High demand for organic, gluten-free options
- **Delivery:** 30-40% of orders via apps (HungerStation, Jahez)
- **Saudi-owned Preference:** Vision 2030 supporting local brands
- **Experiential Dining:** Unique interiors, themed concepts

## Risk Factors

### High Risks
1. **Location Risk:** Wrong location = business failure
2. **Food Safety:** One incident can destroy reputation
3. **Staff Turnover:** High turnover rates (40-60% annually)
4. **Supply Chain:** Ingredient price fluctuations

### Medium Risks
1. **Seasonality:** Ramadan, summer heat affects foot traffic
2. **Competition:** Market saturation in prime areas
3. **Rent Increases:** Common in popular locations
4. **Regulatory Changes:** Health codes, labor laws

### Mitigation Strategies

1. **Location:**
   - Conduct foot traffic analysis
   - Study competitor density
   - Negotiate rent with revenue-sharing clause

2. **Food Safety:**
   - Strict HACCP protocols
   - Regular staff training
   - Quality control systems

3. **Staff Management:**
   - Competitive wages (above market rate)
   - Clear career progression
   - Employee benefits

4. **Supply Chain:**
   - Multiple suppliers
   - Long-term contracts
   - Inventory management system

## Success Factors

## Legal & Compliance

### Saudization (Nitaqat) Requirements

For food & beverage businesses:
- Small (1-5 employees): 1 Saudi employee
- Medium (6-49 employees): 10-20% Saudi
- Large (50+ employees): 20-30% Saudi

**Typical Saudi Positions:**
- Manager/Supervisor
- Cashier
- Customer service
- Marketing/Social media

### Working Hours

- Maximum 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week
- Ramadan: 6 hours/day
- Overtime: 150% of regular wage
- Friday is weekend (or substitute day)

Food & beverage businesses in Saudi Arabia can be highly profitable with proper planning. Key success factors are:

1. Prime location selection
2. Strong concept differentiation
3. Consistent quality
