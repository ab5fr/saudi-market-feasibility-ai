TITLE: ZATCA - Tax and Zakat Registration Guide
AUTHORITY: ZATCA (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority)
URL: https://zatca.gov.sa
DATE: 2024

## Overview
ZATCA is responsible for collecting Zakat and taxes in Saudi Arabia. All businesses must register and comply with Zakat/tax obligations.

## Registration Requirements

### Who Must Register
All businesses must register with ZATCA:
- Saudi-owned companies (Zakat)
- Foreign-owned companies (Tax)
- Mixed ownership (both Zakat and Tax)
- Individual establishments (if applicable)

### Registration Timeline
- **Within 30 days** of obtaining Commercial Registration
- **Before commencing operations** (recommended)

## Zakat vs Tax

### Zakat (for Saudi-owned businesses)
- **Rate:** 2.5% of net worth or taxable income
- **Applicable to:** Saudi citizens and GCC nationals
- **Calculation Base:** Net invested funds or taxable income

### Income Tax (for foreign-owned businesses)
- **Rate:** 20% of net income
- **Applicable to:** Non-Saudi/non-GCC shareholders
- **Withholding Tax:** Varies by type (5%-20%)

### Mixed Ownership
- **Saudi share:** Subject to Zakat
- **Foreign share:** Subject to Income Tax
- **Separate calculations required**

## VAT (Value Added Tax)

### Registration Thresholds
- **Mandatory:** Annual revenue > SAR 375,000
- **Voluntary:** Annual revenue > SAR 187,500
- **Exempt:** Revenue below SAR 187,500 (but can register)

### VAT Rates
- **Standard Rate:** 15%
- **Zero Rate:** 0% (specific exports, certain goods)
- **Exempt:** No VAT (financial services, residential rent, healthcare)

### VAT Filing
- **Frequency:** Monthly or Quarterly (based on revenue)
- **Monthly:** Revenue > SAR 40 million
- **Quarterly:** Revenue < SAR 40 million
- **Deadline:** Last day of month following period end

## Registration Process

### Step 1: Online Registration
1. Visit https://zatca.gov.sa
2. Click "Taxpayer Registration"
3. Enter Commercial Registration number
4. Fill business information
5. Submit required documents

### Step 2: Document Upload
- Commercial Registration
- Bank account details
- Owner/shareholder IDs
- Financial statements (if existing business)
- Lease contract

### Step 3: Verification
- ZATCA reviews application (5-10 business days)
- May request additional documents
- Taxpayer certificate issued

### Step 4: VAT Registration (if applicable)
- Separate application for VAT
- Must register if above threshold
- VAT certificate issued

## Filing and Payment

### Zakat Return
- **Filing Deadline:** 120 days after fiscal year end
- **Payment:** Same as filing deadline
- **Form:** Zakat Declaration Form

### Income Tax Return
- **Filing Deadline:** 120 days after fiscal year end
- **Payment:** Same as filing deadline
- **Form:** Corporate Tax Return

### VAT Return
- **Monthly Filing:** 15th of following month
- **Quarterly Filing:** Last day of month following quarter end
- **Payment:** Same as filing deadline

## Estimated Tax Costs

### Example 1: Saudi-owned LLC (SAR 1M revenue, SAR 200K profit)
- Zakat (2.5% of profit): SAR 5,000
- VAT collected (15% on sales): SAR 150,000
- Less: VAT paid on purchases
- **Net VAT due:** Varies by input VAT

### Example 2: Foreign-owned Branch (SAR 2M revenue, SAR 400K profit)
- Income Tax (20% of profit): SAR 80,000
- VAT obligations: Same calculation

### Example 3: Mixed Ownership (60% Saudi, 40% Foreign)
- Zakat portion (60% of profit × 2.5%)
- Tax portion (40% of profit × 20%)
- VAT obligations: Full amount

## Withholding Tax

### Applicable Payments
- Dividends to non-residents: 5%
- Royalties: 15%
- Technical services: 5%
- Management fees: 20%
- Rent: 5%

### Compliance
- Deduct at time of payment
- File monthly withholding return
- Pay by 10th of following month

## Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late registration | SAR 10,000 |
| Late filing | 1% of tax due per month delay |
| Late payment | 1% of tax due per month delay |
| Non-filing | 25% of tax due |
| Tax evasion | 100% of tax due + criminal prosecution |
| Incorrect VAT invoice | SAR 5,000 - 50,000 |

## E-Invoicing (Fatoorah)

### Requirements
- All VAT-registered businesses must use e-invoicing
- Two phases:
  - **Phase 1:** Generation of electronic invoices
  - **Phase 2:** Integration with ZATCA system

### Technical Requirements
- ZATCA-compliant software
- XML invoice format
- QR code generation
- Digital signature

### Timeline
- Phase 1: Already implemented
- Phase 2: Rolling implementation by business size

## Common Deductions

### Allowable Expenses
- Salaries and wages
- Rent and utilities
- Depreciation
- Marketing expenses
- Professional fees
- Travel expenses (business)
- Insurance

### Non-Deductible Expenses
- Personal expenses
- Fines and penalties
- Entertainment expenses
- Zakat and tax payments

## Record Keeping

### Requirements
- Maintain records for 10 years
- Digital records acceptable
- Must be in Arabic or bilingual
- Include all invoices and receipts

### Recommended Software
- QuickBooks (Arabic version)
- SAP Business One
- Oracle NetSuite
- Local ERP systems

## Tax Incentives

### For Specific Regions
- Certain economic zones offer tax holidays
- Reduced rates for manufacturing
- Export incentives

### For Specific Activities
- R&D tax credits
- Training incentives
- Saudization bonuses (tax deductions)

## Tips for Compliance

1. **Register Early:** Don't wait for 30-day deadline
2. **Use Accounting Software:** Easier tracking and reporting
3. **Hire Tax Consultant:** For complex structures
4. **Keep Good Records:** Essential for audits
5. **File on Time:** Avoid penalties
6. **Understand VAT:** Complex rules require attention
