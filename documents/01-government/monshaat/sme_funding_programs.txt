## Overview
Monsha'at, the General Authority for SMEs, is the government agency responsible for regulating, supporting, developing, and financing the SME sector in Saudi Arabia. SMEs represent 99.5% of business entities in the Kingdom.

## Funding Programs

### 1. Kafalah Program (Guarantee Program)
- **Purpose:** Loan guarantees for SMEs unable to provide collateral
- **Coverage:** Up to 80% of loan amount
- **Maximum Guarantee:** SAR 40 million
- **Eligibility:** SMEs with valid Commercial Registration

### 2. SME Lending Program
- **Purpose:** Direct financing for SME projects
- **Loan Amount:** SAR 100,000 to SAR 10 million
- **Interest Rate:** Competitive rates based on project risk
- **Repayment Period:** Up to 7 years

### 3. Venture Capital Support
- **Purpose:** Equity financing for high-growth startups
- **Focus Sectors:** Technology, manufacturing, services
- **Investment Size:** SAR 2 million to SAR 50 million

## Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for Monsha'at programs:
1. Valid Commercial Registration (CR)
2. Annual revenue less than 375 million SAR
3. Fewer than 250 employees
4. Saudi ownership (varies by program)

## Application Process

1. **Online Registration:** Create account on Monsha'at portal
2. **Document Submission:** Upload required documents
3. **Project Evaluation:** 2-4 weeks review period
4. **Approval & Disbursement:** Upon approval

## Non-Financial Support

- Business incubators and accelerators
- Training and capacity building
- Export support programs
- E-commerce enablement
