Question: What is the capital of Saudi Arabia?
Answer: The capital of Saudi Arabia is Riyadh. Riyadh is a major economic and political hub, serving as the center of government for the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

Question: What are the main industries in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: The main industries in Saudi Arabia include:
1. Oil and Gas - The largest industry, with Saudi Arabia being one of the world's largest oil producers
2. Petrochemicals - Refining and chemical production based on crude oil
3. Construction and Real Estate - Rapid development and urbanization
4. Retail and Commerce - Growing consumer market
5. Finance and Banking - Banking sector is well-developed
6. Tourism - Rapidly developing sector with Vision 2030 investments
7. Manufacturing - Growing industrial base

Question: How do I start a business in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: To start a business in Saudi Arabia, you need to follow these steps:
1. Obtain a Commercial Registration from the Ministry of Commerce
2. Get municipal license from your local municipality (Balady)
3. Register with GOSI for social insurance if you have employees
4. Register with ZATCA for tax and Zakat purposes
5. Open a business bank account
6. Hire employees and issue labor contracts if needed
7. Comply with industry-specific regulations

Question: What is the labor law in Saudi Arabia?
Answer: Saudi Arabia's labor law is governed by the Labor Law for the Private Sector, which includes:
- Maximum working hours: 48 hours per week
- Minimum wage requirements
- Annual leave entitlements: 30 days per year
- Health insurance requirements for employees
- Regulations on foreign workers (Iqama)
- Protection for workers' rights
- Regulations on contract types and termination

Question: What are the licensing requirements for a restaurant?
Answer: For a restaurant in Saudi Arabia, you need:
1. Commercial Registration from Ministry of Commerce
2. Municipal License from local Balady office
3. Health and Safety Certificate from health authorities
4. Food Handler Certificate
5. Environmental Compliance Certificate if required
6. Labor contracts for all employees
7. GOSI registration for social insurance
8. ZATCA tax registration
